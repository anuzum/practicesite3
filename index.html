Experience

  

EXPERIENCE

Beginning (Phase 1)

  

I started out my career in psychology research. After earning my first graduate degree, I worked in the field of counseling, specifically with the foster care community.

Middle (Phase 2)

I then took a career break when I had my children. I quit my job during my maternity leave. Something about becoming a parent made me feel brave enough to want to take risks and think about what I really, truly wanted to do. During my time as a stay-at-home parent, I took on various volunteer roles in which I pursued what I love: writing and editing.

While volunteering and reflecting during my career break, I realized what my ultimate goal was: to return to the field of English, work as a writer/editor, and pursue a graduate degree in English Literature.

Current (Phase 3)

I did debate about whether to go back to school full-time and earn my graduate degree first before going back to work, but I decided to get a job first and then pursue the degree. Because of that decision, I am now busier than I have ever been in my life and barely have time... ever. This has made me extremely intentional with my time and helped me prioritize the things that matter the most to me. Part of this journey has been quitting social media--in doing so, I gave myself hours of my day back and realized I had the capacity to instead prioritize activities that bring my life value and meaning. Here's a video about the perks of not using social media in case you are interested!

Career Goals

 

CAREER GOALS


Short-Term Goals

For my very short-term goals, I'd like to:

  • Figure out HTML and CSS

My current short-term goals are to continue to work full time as an editor for a government contractor while I finish my master's degree in English literature. Given that I am taking one class at a time, this may take me until the summer or fall of 2027.

Long-Term Goals

Long term, my vision is to continue to write so that one day, I can publish a book. I am currently workshopping ideas and have several ideas for children's and young adult books.

In order to make this goal a reality and not just something I have been talking about for decades, I need to carve out more time to write daily. It is difficult to work on this goal because I feel that my short term goals are taking up all of my time. I found this article about writing regularly to complete a novel very helpful and hope to apply these suggestions.

I also began writing a novel in my Novel class that I took in the Spring of 2024 with Professor Ngalabak, and I hope to continue writing that as well. I hope I do not add that to my pile of unfinished writing.

Portfolio

PORTFOLIO

I love writing papers for my English classes! As I mentioned before, I am in my first year in the master's program at GMU for English literature. Writing papers is genuinely fun for me.
